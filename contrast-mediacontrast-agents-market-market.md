<p><p>Contrast media, also known as contrast agents, are substances used in medical imaging to enhance the visibility of internal organs, blood vessels, or tissues. There are several types of contrast media available in the market. Iodinated contrast media contain iodine and are commonly used in X-ray and computed tomography (CT) scans. Gadolinium contrast media are used in magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scans. Barium contrast media are used in gastrointestinal tract imaging. Microbubble contrast media are used in ultrasound imaging to enhance the visibility of blood vessels or organs.</p></p>
