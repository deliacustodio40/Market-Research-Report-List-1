<p><p>The methane market is divided into two main types: fermentation and gasification. In the fermentation market, methane is produced through the breakdown of organic matter by bacteria in anaerobic conditions. This process commonly occurs in landfills and wastewater treatment plants. On the other hand, in the gasification market, methane is derived from the thermal decomposition of carbon-rich materials, such as coal or biomass, in a controlled environment with limited oxygen supply. Both markets contribute to the production of methane, a versatile and widely used energy source.</p></p>
