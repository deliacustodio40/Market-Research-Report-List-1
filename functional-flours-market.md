<p><p>Functional flours are categorized into two main types: cereals and legumes. Cereal-based functional flours are derived from grains such as wheat, corn, rice, and oats. These flours offer various functionalities like binding, thickening, and texturizing in food products. On the other hand, legume-based functional flours are made from legumes like chickpeas, lentils, and peas. These flours are rich in proteins, fibers, and other nutrients, making them suitable for enhancing nutritional values in foods. Both types of functional flours have gained popularity in the food industry due to their versatile applications and potential health benefits.</p></p>
