<p><p>Pharmacodynamic biomarker testing involves evaluating the effect of a drug on the body and determining its efficacy or toxicity. LC-MS (Liquid Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry) is a technique used to separate and analyze drug molecules in a sample, providing information on their concentration and metabolism. MS (Mass Spectrometry) is a method for identifying and quantifying molecules based on their mass-to-charge ratio. Both LC-MS and MS are widely used in pharmacodynamic biomarker testing, enabling precise measurement of drug levels and identification of biomarkers to enhance drug development and patient monitoring.</p></p>
