<p><p>Engine intake filters are essential components that prevent airborne contaminants from entering the engine, ensuring its optimal performance and longevity. Wet type filters use a special oil-coated media to trap and retain particles, providing high filtration efficiency, especially for larger debris. On the other hand, dry type filters use a dense, non-oiled media that relies on static electricity to capture and hold particles. They are generally more popular due to their lower maintenance requirements, ease of installation, and ability to provide efficient filtration for fine particles.</p></p>
