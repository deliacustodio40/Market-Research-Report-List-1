<p><p>Veno-Venous Extracorporeal Life Support (VV ECLS) devices are used in critical care settings to provide temporary heart and lung support to patients with severe respiratory failure. </p><p>Single-ventricular devices are designed to support one side of the heart, typically the left ventricle, by removing oxygen-depleted blood from the body and returning oxygen-rich blood back to the body. </p><p>Biventricular devices, on the other hand, provide support to both sides of the heart by removing and oxygenating blood from the body and then returning it. These devices are crucial in maintaining adequate blood oxygenation and circulation until the patient's lungs and heart recover.</p></p>
