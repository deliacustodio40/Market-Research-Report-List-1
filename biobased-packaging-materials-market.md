<p><p>Biobased packaging materials can be classified into different types including starch-based, cellulose-based, protein-based, and others. Starch-based materials are derived from renewable sources such as corn, wheat, or potatoes and offer good mechanical strength. Cellulose-based materials are made from plant fibers and are known for their high barrier properties against moisture and gases. Protein-based materials are obtained from animal by-products or plant proteins, and they can provide biodegradability and biocompatibility. Other types of biobased packaging materials can include polylactic acid (PLA) or polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA), which are derived from microbial fermentation of renewable resources.</p></p>
