<p><p>Optical tweezers and magnetic tweezers are two types of tools used in the field of mechanobiology. Optical tweezers utilize highly focused laser beams to manipulate small particles, such as cells or microscopic objects, with precision and control. These tweezers are based on the principle of radiation pressure exerted by the laser light. On the other hand, magnetic tweezers employ magnetic fields to manipulate objects or molecules that have been labeled with magnetic beads. Both types of tweezers play crucial roles in various biophysical experiments and studies conducted in the field of mechanobiology.</p></p>
