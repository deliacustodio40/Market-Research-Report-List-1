<p><p>Modified Atmospheric Packaging (MAP) refers to a technique used to extend the shelf life of food products by altering the atmosphere inside the packaging. There are various types of MAP, including Passive MAP, Active MAP, and Others. Passive MAP involves the removal of oxygen from the packaging, reducing spoilage caused by oxidation. Active MAP, on the other hand, introduces gases such as carbon dioxide and nitrogen to inhibit the growth of bacteria. The Others category includes advanced MAP methods like vacuum packaging and high-pressure processing, which offer additional benefits in food preservation and quality.</p></p>
