<p><p>The cathode plate market finds application in electrolytic refining (ER) and electrodeposition (ED) processes. Electrolytic refining involves separating metal impurities from raw materials through an electrochemical process, with the cathode plate serving as the negatively charged electrode. On the other hand, electrodeposition refers to the deposition of metal coatings onto a substrate through an electrochemical process, with the cathode plate acting as the target surface for the metal ions to be deposited. In both cases, the cathode plate plays a crucial role in the efficient and precise extraction or deposition of metals.</p></p>
