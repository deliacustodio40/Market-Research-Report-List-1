<p><p>Viscosity modifiers are substances used to enhance or control the viscosity of fluids. There are various types of viscosity modifiers available on the market. Minerals, such as clays or talc, are commonly used viscosity modifiers due to their ability to thicken liquids. Hydrocolloids, such as xanthan gum or guar gum, are natural additives that increase viscosity by forming a gel or thickening solution. Synthetic polymers, such as polyethylene oxide or polyacrylamide, are created through chemical processes and are commonly used as viscosity modifiers. Besides these categories, there are also various other types of viscosity modifiers available in the market, each offering specific benefits and properties.</p></p>
