<p><p>The single-use laparoscopic retriever market is divided into two types: detachable and non-detachable. Detachable retrievers are designed with a mechanism that allows the retrieval end to be separated from the handle for easy removal of specimen. On the other hand, non-detachable retrievers have a fixed retrieval end that cannot be separated from the handle. Both types are used in minimally invasive laparoscopic surgeries to retrieve samples or remove tissue from the patient's body. However, detachable retrievers offer the advantage of easier specimen removal compared to non-detachable ones.</p></p>
