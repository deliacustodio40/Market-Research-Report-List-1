With the growing concerns regarding environmental sustainability, manufacturers are focusing on developing nanoclays using renewable resources such as starch and cellulose. <p><p>Nanoclays are a type of nanomaterials that have unique properties due to their small size and large surface area. There are three main types of nanoclays based on their composition: kaolinite, smectite, and other market variants. Kaolinite nanoclays are derived from the mineral kaolin and are known for their high thermal stability and electrical insulation. Smectite nanoclays, on the other hand, are made from minerals like montmorillonite and exhibit excellent absorbency and swelling properties. The "other market" category includes nanoclays derived from minerals such as halloysite or illite and may have their own distinct characteristics.</p></p>
