<p><p>Biological pesticides, also known as biopesticides, are derived from natural sources such as animals, plants, bacteria, and certain minerals. They are used to control pests and diseases in agriculture, forestry, and public health without harming the environment or human health. Unlike conventional pesticides, biological pesticides target specific pests, reducing the risk of non-target organisms being affected.</p><p>The global biological pesticide market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years due to several factors. The increasing demand for organic food products, growing awareness regarding environmental safety, and the ban on chemical pesticides in several countries have contributed to the market expansion. There is a shift towards the development of novel and improved biopesticides, particularly those that target specific pests and have higher efficacy. Microbial biopesticides, such as Bacillus thuringiensis and Trichoderma spp., are gaining popularity due to their effectiveness against a wide range of pests and diseases. In addition, the integration of biological pesticides with conventional chemical pesticides for enhanced pest control is emerging as a prominent trend in the market.</p><p>Geographically, North America and Europe hold a significant share in the biological pesticide market, owing to the stringent regulations on chemical pesticide usage and the increasing demand for organic food. However, the Asia-Pacific region is expected to exhibit the highest growth rate due to the rising population, growing agricultural practices, and the adoption of sustainable farming methods.</p><p>Overall, the biological pesticide market is poised for substantial growth in the coming years, driven by the increasing preference for organic and sustainable agricultural practices, stricter regulations on chemical pesticides, and the development of innovative biopesticides.</p></p>
