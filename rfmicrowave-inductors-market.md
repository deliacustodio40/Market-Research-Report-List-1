<p><p>RF/Microwave inductors are crucial components in various electronic devices and systems. There are three main types of RF/Microwave inductors available in the market: wire wound type, film type, and multilayer type. Wire wound inductors are made by winding a wire around a core material, while film inductors use a thin film that is deposited on a substrate. Multilayer inductors consist of multiple layers of conductive material sandwiched between insulating layers. Each type has its own advantages and applications, catering to the specific needs of different electronic circuits and systems.</p></p>
