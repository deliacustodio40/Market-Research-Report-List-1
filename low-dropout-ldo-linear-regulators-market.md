<p><ul><li>PMOS</li><li>NMOS</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Low Dropout (LDO) linear regulators are a type of voltage regulator used to maintain a stable output voltage from a varying input voltage. LDOs come in various market types, including PMOS and NMOS. PMOS refers to LDOs that use P-channel MOSFETs for voltage regulation. These regulators are known for their high precision and low quiescent current consumption. On the other hand, NMOS LDOs utilize N-channel MOSFETs for voltage regulation. NMOS LDOs are highly efficient and offer better power handling capabilities, making them suitable for high-current applications. Both PMOS and NMOS LDOs play significant roles in providing reliable voltage regulation solutions.</p></p>
