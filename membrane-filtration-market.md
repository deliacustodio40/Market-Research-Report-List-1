<p><p>The membrane filtration market consists of various types of membranes used for filtration purposes. Reverse Osmosis (RO) membranes are designed to remove ions, particles, and larger molecules from water by applying pressure. Ultrafiltration (UF) membranes are used for separating larger molecules and colloids from water and other fluids. Microfiltration (MF) membranes primarily remove suspended solids and bacteria. Nano-Filtration (NF) membranes remove divalent ions, such as calcium and magnesium, while allowing monovalent ions to pass through. Additionally, there are other types of filtration membranes available in the market, each suited for specific applications and filtration needs.</p></p>
