<p><p>Intrauterine Contraceptive Devices (IUCDs) are small devices that are placed inside the uterus to prevent pregnancy. There are three main types of IUCDs available in the market: non-hormonal, hormonal, and inert. Non-hormonal IUCDs, such as copper IUDs, work by creating a hostile environment for sperm. Hormonal IUCDs, such as levonorgestrel-releasing IUDs, release hormones that prevent fertilization and implantation of an embryo. Inert IUCDs, such as the copper-free IUD, do not release any hormones or affect the reproductive system. These different types offer women a range of choices for contraception.</p></p>
