<p><p>Corn silage inoculants are additives used to enhance the fermentation process of corn silage, improving its quality as animal feed. Inoculants can be classified into two main types: heterofermentative and homofermentative. Heterofermentative inoculants convert sugars into various byproducts, such as lactic acid, acetic acid, ethanol, and carbon dioxide. This type of inoculant aids in reducing dry matter losses and improving aerobic stability. On the other hand, homofermentative inoculants primarily produce lactic acid during fermentation, enhancing the preservation of corn silage by reducing spoilage and improving intake.</p></p>
