<p><p>Expansive cement, also known as shrinkage-compensating cement, is a type of cement that expands as it sets, allowing for more precise and durable construction in various applications. <p><p>Expansive cement is a type of cement designed to expand during its setting period. Three market types of expansive cement include K Type, M Type, and S Type. K Type is primarily used in construction projects that require controlled expansion, such as concrete pipes and concrete repairs. M Type is used for applications that demand moderate expansion, including sealing joints in concrete structures. Meanwhile, S Type is ideal for projects requiring significant expansion, such as repair of concrete pavements and injection grouting. Each market type caters to specific construction needs based on the desired level of expansion.</p></p>
