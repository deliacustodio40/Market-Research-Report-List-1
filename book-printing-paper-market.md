<p><p>Book printing paper market types can be categorized into uncoated and coated paper. Uncoated paper is porous and absorbs ink, making it suitable for text-heavy books. It has a natural look and feel but lacks smoothness and brightness. Coated paper, on the other hand, has a glossy or matte finish due to a coating of clay or chemicals. This type is ideal for books with images or illustrations, as it provides vibrant colors and sharper details. Coated paper offers better print quality but is generally more expensive than uncoated paper.</p></p>
