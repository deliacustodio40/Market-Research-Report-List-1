<p><p>Solenoid valves for semiconductors are classified based on the number of ports they have and their operation type. Two-way solenoid valves have an inlet and an outlet and control the flow of liquid or gas in a single direction. Three-way solenoid valves have an additional port to divert the flow. By product type, they can be further categorized as direct-acting or pilot-operated valves. Normally closed solenoid valves block the flow in their resting state and open when energized, while normally open valves allow flow in their resting state and close when energized.</p></p>
